The Athena Bookmarklet

Drag the button below to your bookmarks bar. After that, whenever you're in an Athena post, one click grabs the article text and sends it straight to Headline Lab — no copying, no switching tabs, no pasting.

If you click the bookmarklet while on any other site, it will open Headline Lab directly so you can paste text in manually.

How To Install
  1. Show your bookmarks bar if it's hidden — in Chrome: Ctrl+Shift+B (Win) or ⌘+Shift+B (Mac). In Firefox: View → Toolbars → Bookmarks Toolbar.
  2. Drag the red button above into your bookmarks bar. You'll see it appear as "📰 Headline Lab".
  3. That's it. Go to any Athena post, click the bookmark, and the tool opens with your article text already loaded. On any other site, it opens Headline Lab so you can paste text in manually.
What It Does

When you click the bookmarklet, it checks what site you're on:

  1. On Athena or a GovExec network site — reads the Content field via the CKEditor API, strips HTML tags, grabs the Title and Subhed fields, and POSTs everything directly to Headline Lab, which opens in a new tab with everything pre-filled.
  2. On any other site — opens Headline Lab directly so you can paste text in manually.
Troubleshooting
  • The bookmarklet opens Headline Lab but the text field is empty.
    Make sure you're on an Athena post editing page (the URL should contain /athena/post_manager/post/). The bookmarklet reads the Content field via CKEditor — if you click it from a list page or dashboard, there's no content to grab. Also make sure the post has some body text already entered.
  • It only grabbed the title, not the article body.
    The CKEditor instance may not have loaded yet. Wait a second after the page finishes loading, then try again. If it still fails, let me know — I can tune the selector.
  • Can I see the bookmarklet code?
    Yes — right-click the bookmark in your bar → Edit, and the full JavaScript is visible. It's self-contained, runs only in your browser, and sends text only to prefill.php on this server, which immediately hands it to the tool and discards it.
